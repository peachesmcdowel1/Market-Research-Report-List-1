<p><p>The Dermatology Drug Market can be classified into three types based on the mode of administration: External Use, Oral, and Injection. </p><p>1. External Use: This type includes drugs that are applied topically on the skin, such as creams, ointments, lotions, and gels. These medications work directly on the affected area to treat various skin conditions.</p><p>2. Oral: Drugs in this category are taken orally, usually in the form of tablets or capsules. They are absorbed into the bloodstream and circulate throughout the body, treating systemic skin conditions or providing a more comprehensive approach to dermatological treatment.</p><p>3. Injection: Dermatological drugs in this market segment are administered through injections, typically via intravenous or subcutaneous routes. Injection-based treatments are helpful in managing severe skin conditions or delivering medications directly into the bloodstream for faster and targeted action.</p></p>
