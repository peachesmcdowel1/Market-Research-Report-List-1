<p><p>Close-In Weapon Systems (CIWS) are used for defense against short-range threats such as missiles, aircraft, and small boats. The two main types of CIWS are gun-based systems and missile-based systems. Gun-based CIWS use rapid-firing cannons to intercept and destroy incoming threats, while missile-based CIWS utilize guided missiles for the same purpose. Gun-based systems are typically more cost-effective and have a higher rate of fire, while missile-based systems offer longer range and greater precision in targeting threats. Both types are essential for effectively defending against close-range threats.</p></p>
