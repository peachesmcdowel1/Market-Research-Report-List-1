<p><p>Proximity sensors are widely used in the food market for various applications. Inductive proximity sensors are commonly used for detecting metallic objects, such as cans or metal containers. Capacitive proximity sensors are used for detecting non-metallic objects, such as plastic packaging or liquids. Magnetic proximity sensors are utilized for detecting magnetic objects, such as magnets in packaging or metal lids. These sensors play a crucial role in ensuring accurate detection and sorting of food products in different types of packaging materials.</p></p>
