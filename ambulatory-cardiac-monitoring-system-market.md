<p><p>Ambulatory cardiac monitoring systems are devices used to continuously monitor a patient's heart activity outside of a hospital or clinical setting. The market for these systems can be classified into four types. Holter monitors are portable devices that record the heart's electrical activity for 24 to 48 hours. Event monitors are typically worn for weeks or months and record heart activity during symptomatic events. Mobile cardiac telemetry involves wearing a device that continuously monitors and transmits heart activity for an extended period. Lastly, other types include patch monitors and implantable loop recorders.</p></p>
