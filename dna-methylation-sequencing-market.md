<p><strong>The DNA Methylation Sequencing Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Whole Genome Bisulfite Sequencing (WGBS)</li><li>Reduced Representation Bisulfite Sequencing (RRBS)</li><li>MeDIP-Sequncing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>DNA methylation sequencing is a technique used to study the methylation patterns in DNA molecules. The market for this sequencing technique includes three key types. Whole Genome Bisulfite Sequencing (WGBS) offers a comprehensive analysis of DNA methylation patterns by mapping modifications across the entire genome. Reduced Representation Bisulfite Sequencing (RRBS) focuses on a subset of the genome, increasing cost-effectiveness while maintaining good coverage. MeDIP-Sequencing combines immunoprecipitation and sequencing to identify regions of methylated DNA. These techniques enable researchers to understand DNA methylation patterns and their impact on gene expression and disease development.</p></p>
