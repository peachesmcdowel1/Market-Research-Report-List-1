<p><p>Low-E insulated glass is available in two main types: hard-coated and soft-coated. Hard-coated Low-E glass features a durable coating applied during the manufacturing process, making it better suited for harsh environments and high-traffic areas. Soft-coated Low-E glass, on the other hand, has a more delicate coating applied after the glass is produced, providing superior energy efficiency and thermal performance. Both types of Low-E insulated glass offer benefits in terms of reducing heat transfer, improving energy savings, and enhancing overall comfort in buildings.</p></p>
