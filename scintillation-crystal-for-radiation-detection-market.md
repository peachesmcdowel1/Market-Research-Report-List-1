<p><p>Scintillation crystals are used in radiation detection to convert the energy of incoming radiation into visible light. There are two main types of scintillation crystals: organic crystals and inorganic crystals. Organic crystals are typically made of aromatic hydrocarbons and offer fast response times, while inorganic crystals, such as sodium iodide or bismuth germanate, provide high light output and energy resolution. Both types of crystals have their own unique advantages and are utilized in various applications within the radiation detection market.</p></p>
