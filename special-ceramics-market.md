<p><p>Special ceramics are classified into different types based on the materials used in their composition. Oxide ceramics, such as alumina and zirconia, are commonly used for their excellent electrical and thermal insulating properties. Nitride ceramics, like silicon nitride, offer superior mechanical strength and thermal shock resistance. Carbide ceramics, such as silicon carbide, are highly wear-resistant materials. Boride ceramics, like titanium diboride, exhibit high hardness and chemical resistance. Other types of special ceramics include composites and hybrid materials that possess unique properties suited for specific applications.</p></p>
