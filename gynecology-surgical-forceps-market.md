<p><p>Gynecology Surgical Forceps refer to instruments used in gynecological surgery to grasp, manipulate, or remove tissues. There are three main types: Allis Forceps, Artery Forceps, and Others. Allis Forceps have serrated jaws and are employed to hold tissues during procedures. Artery Forceps, also known as hemostatic forceps, are used to control bleeding by clamping blood vessels. Other forceps include Vulsellum Forceps, used to grasp the cervix, and Sponge Holding Forceps, used to hold sponges or gauze. Each type plays a crucial role in facilitating gynecological surgeries.</p></p>
