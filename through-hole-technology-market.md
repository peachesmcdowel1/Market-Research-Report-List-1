<p><p>Through-hole technology refers to a method of assembling electronic components by inserting leads into holes on a printed circuit board (PCB) and soldering them in place. In the market, there are different types of through-hole components, such as wirewound and axial types. Wirewound components are designed with a wire wound around a core to create a magnetic field. They are commonly used in applications that require high power or high precision. Axial components have leads that extend from both ends and are used for various purposes like resistors or capacitors. These components offer stability and reliability in electronic circuits.</p></p>
