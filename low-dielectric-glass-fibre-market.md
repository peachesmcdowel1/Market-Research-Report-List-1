<p><p>The low dielectric glass fibre market is divided into two main types: D-Glass Fiber and NE-Glass Fiber. D-Glass Fiber refers to a type of glass fibre that has the lowest dielectric constant, making it suitable for applications where low electrical conductivity is required, such as in electrical insulation. On the other hand, NE-Glass Fiber, also known as non-alkaline glass fibre, possesses a lower alkali content, making it more resistant to corrosion and able to withstand higher temperatures. Both types of glass fibre have unique properties that make them ideal for different industrial applications.</p></p>
