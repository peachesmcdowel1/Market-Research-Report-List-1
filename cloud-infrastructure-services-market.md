<p><p>Public Cloud refers to a type of cloud infrastructure service where resources are shared among multiple users over the internet. Private Cloud, on the other hand, provides dedicated resources to a single organization either on-premises or through a third-party provider. Hybrid Cloud combines the features of both public and private clouds, allowing organizations to leverage the benefits of both models. Each market type offers different levels of control, security, and flexibility to meet the diverse needs of businesses.</p></p>
