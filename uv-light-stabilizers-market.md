<p><p>UV light stabilizers are additives used in plastics, coatings, and other materials to protect them from the harmful effects of ultraviolet radiation. There are three main types of UV light stabilizers: UV absorbers, which absorb UV radiation and convert it into harmless heat; hindered amine light stabilizers (HALS), which act as free radical scavengers; and benzoates, which function by absorbing UV radiation and dissipating it as heat. Each type of stabilizer offers unique benefits depending on the specific application and material being protected.</p></p>
