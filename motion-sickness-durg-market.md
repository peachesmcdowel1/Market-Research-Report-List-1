<p><p>Motion sickness drugs can be classified into various types based on their mechanisms of action. Anticholinergic drugs work by blocking the action of a neurotransmitter, acetylcholine, in the brain to reduce motion sickness symptoms. Antihistamines, on the other hand, block histamine receptors in the brain, which helps to alleviate nausea and vomiting. Other types of motion sickness drugs may work through different mechanisms such as acting on serotonin receptors or targeting the vestibular system.</p></p>
