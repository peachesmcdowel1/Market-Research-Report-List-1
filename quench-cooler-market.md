<p><ul><li>Liquid Phase Quenching</li><li>Air Quenching</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Quench Cooler market types include Liquid Phase Quenching and Air Quenching. In Liquid Phase Quenching, a liquid medium, such as a quench oil or water, is used to rapidly cool the heated material, ensuring high hardness and strength. This method is commonly employed in industries like automotive and manufacturing. On the other hand, Air Quenching involves exposing the hot material to ambient air for cooling. This method is more cost-effective and is often used for smaller components and applications that do not require extreme hardness.</p></p>
