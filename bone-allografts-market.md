<p><p>Bone allografts are grafts used in bone transplantation procedures. Synthetic grafts are artificially created materials that mimic the properties of natural bone. Allografts are bone grafts obtained from human donors, typically from deceased individuals. Xenografts are grafts sourced from animals, such as cows or pigs, and are treated to remove any potential harmful components. Other types of bone allografts may include grafts made from ceramics or polymers. These different types provide options for surgeons to choose the most suitable graft material for each patient's specific needs.</p></p>
