<p><ul><li>Low Field Strength</li><li>Mid Field Strength</li><li>High Field Strength</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Medical Nuclear Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) Scanners are classified based on their field strength levels. Low Field Strength scanners operate at 0.23T to 0.3T, Mid Field Strength scanners operate at 0.5T to 1.5T, and High Field Strength scanners operate at 3T and above. Low Field Strength scanners are cost-effective and commonly used for extremity imaging. Mid Field Strength scanners are versatile and widely used for various imaging needs. High Field Strength scanners provide high-resolution images and are ideal for research and complex imaging procedures.</p></p>
