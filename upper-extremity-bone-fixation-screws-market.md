<p><p>Upper extremity bone fixation screws are devices used in orthopedic surgeries to stabilize bone fractures. These screws come in three main types: stainless-steel, titanium, and bioabsorbable. Stainless-steel screws are known for their strength and durability, making them suitable for long-term fixation. Titanium screws are lighter in weight and have good biocompatibility, reducing the risk of reactions. Bioabsorbable screws gradually dissolve in the body over time, eliminating the need for a second surgery to remove the screws. Each type has its own advantages and applications in orthopedic surgery.</p></p>
