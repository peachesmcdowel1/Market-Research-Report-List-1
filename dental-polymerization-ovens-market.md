<p><p>Dental polymerization ovens are used to cure and harden dental materials like composites and ceramics. There are several types of ovens available in the market: muffle, vacuum, infrared, microwave, and others. Muffle ovens use a heating chamber surrounded by heating elements to evenly distribute heat. Vacuum ovens remove air to prevent bubble formation in the materials. Infrared ovens use infrared radiation to cure the materials. Microwave ovens utilize microwave energy for faster polymerization. Other types include those with unique designs or combinations of different heating methods to cater to specific curing needs in the dental industry.</p></p>
